### Clear limits in the bylaws

Under a proposed set of bylaws, the association’s purpose would be explicitly limited to:

- Fostering communication among residents  
- Organizing voluntary, community‑building activities  
- Making non‑binding recommendations to public agencies on issues referred by members  

***

### The bylaws would state:

“The Association has no regulatory, enforcement, or quasi‑judicial authority over residents or property. Any guidelines or positions adopted by the Association are advisory only and not binding on residents or property owners.”

To prevent a small executive group from acting unilaterally, the bylaws would:

- Require Board or member approval for significant actions (public positions, contracts, grants, spending above a set threshold).  
- State that officers have no authority to bind the Association except as expressly authorized.  
- Keep final authority with the membership as a whole, not just the elected executives.

***

### 60% household membership goal

To address concerns about a small group acting without broad representation, we propose a target of membership from at least **60% of households**. This is intentionally above the common 50% threshold many cities use to consider a residents’ association “representative” of its area.

Membership would be:

- **Individual**, not per household  
- Based on a **one‑person, one‑vote** principle  
- Open to both owners and renters  

With two membership categories:

- **Adult Members (18+)**: full voting rights and eligibility for office.  
- **Youth Members (under 18)**: full participation in meetings, discussions, and committees, with a deliberate focus on civic learning and engagement.

This approach specifically avoids “one vote per household” models, which have historically limited whose voice is counted within the community.

***

### Transparent process for City submissions

A third element of the framework is a clear, transparent process for how ideas would be gathered and brought to the City:

- Multiple channels for input (online form, email, surveys, open meetings, suggestion boxes).  
- A standing committee to review submissions, assess resident support, and short‑list items.  
- Required Board or member approval before any formal submission to the City.  
- Public summaries of:
  - What was heard from residents  
  - How that input shaped the association’s position  
  - Any response received from the City  

The overall intent is to design an association that is **legally constrained to its intended role** and **demonstrably representative** of the neighbourhood it serves.
